Navigating a phone with a dead screen is hard. Here are tricks to help you find your way.

## ðŸ—£ï¸ The "Golden Ticket": TalkBack
If you can enable TalkBack (Screen Reader), the phone will **tell you** what you are selecting.
*   **Shortcut**: Press `Win+Alt+Enter` (sometimes `Ctrl+Alt+Enter` or Hold `Volume Up + Volume Down` for 3 seconds).
*   **If enabled**: You will hear "TalkBack On".
*   **Navigation changes**:
    *   Use `Alt+Right` / `Alt+Left` to move focus.
    *   Use `Alt+Enter` to click.

## âŒ¨ï¸ Common Keyboard Shortcuts
*   **Wake Up**: Press `Space` or `Power Key` (mapped to a key if your keyboard supports it, otherwise `Space` usually wakes).
*   **Unlock**:
    *   Swipe Up: Drag mouse up OR press `Space` then `Tab` then `Enter`.
    *   PIN: Type numbers on Numpad. Enter.
*   **Go Home**: `Win+Enter` or `Esc` multiple times.
*   **Notifications**: `Win+N`.
*   **Settings**: `Win+N`, then Right Arrow until you hit the gear icon (usually top right).

## ðŸ§­ Navigating Settings Blindly (Generic Android)
1.  **Open Settings**: Trigger Assistant (`Win`) and type "Open Developer Options" (might fail if not enabled yet) or "Open Settings".
2.  **Scroll to Bottom**: Press `End` key.
    *   *Goal: "About Phone" is usually the last or second-to-last item.*
3.  **Enter "About Phone"**: Press `Up Arrow` once or twice. Press `Enter`.
4.  **Find Build Number**:
    *   It is often at the very bottom. Press `End`.
    *   Press `Enter` 7 times efficiently.
5.  **Go Back**: `Esc`.
6.  **Find Developer Options**:
    *   It is often above "About Phone" or inside "System".
    *   Try `Up Arrow` -> `Enter`.
7.  **Enable USB Debugging**:
    *   This is usually a toggle about 5-10 items down the list.
    *   Press `Down Arrow` roughly 5-10 times.
    *   Press `Enter` to toggle.
    *   **The Trap**: A popup appears "Allow USB Debugging?".
    *   Press `Tab` or `Right Arrow` to move focus to "Allow/OK".
    *   Press `Enter`.
